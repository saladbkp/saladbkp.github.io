The Data Comparison
Look at the two distinct states your script needs to separate:

  • State A (Network Latency Only): This is the “Fast” state. Your logs showed this is consistently around 0.24s.
  • State B (Network + Calculation): This is the “Slow” state.
    • At 50,000 (typo in your question?), the time was 0.24s. (Identical to State A).
    • At 500,000, the time was 0.93s. (Distinct from State A).

The Visualization

Imagine the timeline of a request. You need to draw a line (Threshold) that separates the two states without touching either one.

Scenario 1: Bad Parameters (Gadget = 50,000)

Plaintext

  • Why it fails: The “Slow” calculation finishes so fast that it hides inside the network latency. You cannot draw a line to separate them.

Scenario 2: Perfect Parameters (Gadget = 500,000)

Plaintext

  • Why it works:

    • Fast responses land at 0.24s.

    • Slow responses land at 0.93s.

    • The Gap is huge (~0.7s).

    • Threshold (0.6s) sits perfectly in the empty space (the “Gap”).

Why 0.6?

If you chose a threshold like 0.3s, a small network spike (jitter) could accidentally push a Fast request to 0.31s, causing a false positive. If you chose 0.8s, a slightly faster CPU cycle could drop a Slow request to 0.79s, causing a false negative. 0.6s is the mathematical “safe haven” right in the middle of 0.24 and 0.93.
